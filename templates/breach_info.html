Breach Information

Learn about data breaches and how to handle them. Below is a detailed analysis of some of the largest data breaches since 2010, along with their consequences and financial impacts.

Largest Data Breaches Since 2010 (Chronological Order)

Breach 1: Sony PlayStation Network – April 2011

Key Details:

  • 77 million accounts compromised (names, addresses, email addresses, birthdates, PSN login credentials, purchase history)
  • Credit card information potentially exposed (Sony claims it was encrypted)

Impact & Consequences:

  • 23-day shutdown of PlayStation Network
  • Legal fees, security upgrades, compensation ($171 million)
  • Lawsuits for negligence in protecting user data
  • Loss of customer trust
  • Regulatory scrutiny
  • Reputational damage

Breach 2: Yahoo – August 2013 & November 2014

Key Details:

  • Both breaches combined compromised 3.5 billion accounts (names, email addresses, birthdays, hashed passwords, security questions)
  • 2013 breach not publicly disclosed until September of 2016
  • 2014 breach not publicly disclosed until December of 2016

Impact & Consequences:

  • Reduced acquisition offer from Verizon ($350 million)
  • Settlements, fines ($153.5 million)
  • Loss of customer trust
  • Regulatory scrutiny
  • Reputational damage
  • Security upgrades

Breach 3: Adobe – October 2013

Key Details:

  • 153 million accounts compromised (email addresses, encrypted passwords, password hints, partial payment data)

Impact & Consequences:

  • Lawsuits, settlements ($1.1 million settlement)
  • Loss of customer trust
  • Regulatory scrutiny
  • Reputational damage
  • Security upgrades

Breach 4: Target – December 2013

Key Details:

  • Credit and debit card information from 40 million customers exposed
  • Personal data from 70 million additional individuals exposed (names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses)

Impact & Consequences:

  • Settlements, legal fees, security upgrades ($292 million)
  • Loss of customer trust
  • Regulatory scrutiny
  • Reputational damage

Breach 5: Marriott – June 2014

Key Details:

  • Personal data from 500 million guests compromised (names, phone numbers, email addresses, passport numbers, arrival and departure dates, and encrypted payment card details)

Impact & Consequences:

  • Regulatory lawsuits, fines ($23.8 million)
  • Loss of customer trust
  • Regulatory scrutiny
  • Reputational damage
  • Security Upgrades

Breach 6: Uber – November 2016

Key Details:

  • Data from 57 million users compromised (names, email addresses, phone numbers)
  • 600,000 US drivers had their license numbers exposed (along with their user data)
  • Uber failed to disclose the breach for over a year

Impact & Consequences:

  • Settlement ($148 million)
  • Uber’s former CSO was charged by the US Department of Justice for covering up the breach (convicted of obstruction of justice)
  • Loss of customer trust
  • Regulatory scrutiny
  • Reputational damage
  • Security upgrades

Breach 7: Equifax – July 2017

Key Details:

  • 147 million people had their data compromised (full names, social security numbers, birthdays, addresses, driver’s license numbers, credit card numbers, dispute documents)

Impact & Consequences:

  • Lawsuits, settlements ($700 million)
  • Security upgrades, breach response ($1.4 billion)
  • CEO resignation
  • Loss of customer trust
  • Regulatory scrutiny
  • Reputational damage

Breach 8: Facebook – April 2019

Key Details:

  • Unsecured databases exposed sensitive data from 540 million user accounts (user IDs, account names, comments, likes, reactions, friends lists, interests, check-ins, activity logs)
  • In rare cases, plaintext Facebook passwords were exposed (22,000 users)

Impact & Consequences:

  • Loss of customer trust
  • Regulatory scrutiny
  • Reputational damage
  • Security upgrades

Breach 9: Capital One – July 2019

Key Details:

  • 106 million accounts compromised (names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, birthdays, credit scores, transaction data)
  • Exposed 80,000 bank account numbers, 140,000 US Social Security numbers, and 1 million Canadian Social Insurance numbers

Impact & Consequences:

  • Fines, settlements, lawsuits, security upgrades ($300-500 million)
  • Loss of customer trust
  • Regulatory scrutiny
  • Reputational damage

Breach 10: T-Mobile – August 2021

Key Details:

  • Data from 76.6 million customers compromised (names, birthdays, social security numbers, driver’s license and ID numbers, phone numbers, T-Mobile account details, T-Mobile account PINs)

Impact & Consequences:

  • Settlements ($350 million and T-Mobile required to provide free credit monitoring to all affected individuals)
  • Security upgrades ($150 million)
  • Loss of customer trust
  • Regulatory scrutiny
  • Reputational damage
